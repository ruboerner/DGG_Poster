Open Source Software

Open Source Software refers to software whose source code is freely available to users. This means that anyone can view, modify, and distribute the code as per the terms of the respective license.

Characteristics

  • Transparency: The source code of OSS is openly accessible, allowing users to inspect and understand how the software functions.
  • Collaboration: OSS encourages collaboration among developers worldwide. Anyone can contribute to the improvement of the software by fixing bugs, adding features, or suggesting enhancements.
  • Community-driven Development: OSS projects are often developed and maintained by a community of volunteers or organizations. This community aspect fosters innovation and rapid development.
  • Licensing: OSS is typically distributed under licenses approved by the Open Source Initiative (OSI), such as the GNU General Public License (GPL) or the Apache License. These licenses ensure that the software remains open and can be freely used, modified, and distributed by anyone.
  • Flexibility: Users have the freedom to customize and adapt OSS to suit their specific needs without restrictions imposed by proprietary software.
